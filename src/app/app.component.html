Introduction

Hex Journal is google sheet data base interactive Hex crypto journal where you the user can collect in a spreadsheet format day to day information about Hex. Note down, evaluate day entry points of your liking. Remember the days Hex did I run up, or a special event that may of pumped or dumped Hex. With Hex Journal you have a personal digitized journal that belongs to you to keep track of your Hex t-shares and much more. Unravel the mechanical nature of how this programable product operates. By manually typing in the amount of T-shares you own Hex Journal locks in the current data at time of entry, and displays a past history of earlier entries. This layout gives the user a overview of the functionality of Hex. Your spreadsheet reveals Hex in relationship to market pressure, showing its evolution in terms of adoption. The design of this digital journal connected to API makes comparing past performance with today performance for better future entry points. You the investor can understand with ease the complex nature of Hex through analysis of Hex in Uniswap version 2 and 3 brought and sold by long term and short term investors. Watch the movements of decentralized markets. Understand liquidity, volume and price fluctuations in a digital age of mad gains.

Description

1

The google sheet has "4" pages ( API,About, Hex Journal and Charts). The "API page" is where the data is pulled from (The development resource page from hex.com). The "about page" explains terms, formulas,notes and how to navigate the journal for optimization. There are a explanations on how to use the columns and rows in a number format.

2

The "Journal page" can expand and contract vertically and horizontally. There are 55 columns with 16 data entries. The table is divided into 3 sections. Part "1" extents from 1 to 19 with two expansions which deal which the Hex product. Sections "1" displays T-shares, Hex operations and Hex price. Your T-shares is like a factory thats keeps producing more of itself. The more T-shares you own the more Hex interest you gain. The only input needed to tract your T-shares is the number the user writes (column 1,8). This "number method" avoids the exposure of your contract address by converting it into a number formate. The first expansion shows your personal T-shares performance (1-8).

3

The second expansion shows the collective over all performance of T-shares in collective pool. In this section the share rate shows that Hex share rate increases over time making it impossible to outperform long-term staking with a strategy of frequent short-time staking. Column 14 is total staking in pool with column 15 as allocated supply and the rest follows as the collective percentage in staking pool. The last columns 45-55 is a simulation of hypotheticals with 4 entry points. In column "AU18" you can experiment with APY which collects data from "AU5. Put number of T-shares you desire to speculate "number of days in column AU18". In overview terms, definitions and charts are located far right.

4

Column "A" is your personal note section where user interacts with Hex Journal evaluation. Write down special events concerning that day. Note down your fundamental and technical analysis.

5

Row "2" in the About section are google notes that explains the headers. Google notes are discrete in size and are hard to see, you can find them on all cells in row "2" on top of right corner.

6

Row "3" is where links are found created by the Hex and the uniswap community. With just one click the user can link directly without doing unnecessary time consuming work. Uniswap links retrieve data source form uniswap version 2-3. Entry points must be manually executed.

1

Hex Journal "About sheet" underlines basic layout and functionality of how to access the Journal.

2

Column "43" is Hex ratio between liquidity and volume trading pairs. The lower the number the higher the volume. The higher the number the lower the volume.

3

Column "43" is the ratio between Uniswap 1-2-3 Total liquidity and volume with all erc-20 trading pairs. The lower the number the higher the volume. The higher the number the lower the volume.

Column "1" to "18" is the inner workings of Hex. Column "1" is amount of your T-shares (yours). The dark columns is for input use that is done manually, and where totals are shown.

Column "27" to "44" is uniswap data, this section must be filled manually. This section is optional.

Columns 45-55 display the definitions with number count. The number count looks at the chart overview found in AV10-52. The number count method prevent user from scrolling 45 columns horizontally, which is time consuming. Column AU18 is where the days go for estimated T-shares earning per T-shares written on column AU5 in the simulation. There are 4 simulations with 4 rows of input data points to experiment with. Pink headers is for input use. There are 8 overview charts the first 3 charts, T-shares and price. The remaining 5 charts is Hex in relation to Uniswap.
