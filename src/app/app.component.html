Introduction

Hex Journal is google sheet data base interactive Hex crypto journal where you the user can collect in a spread sheet format day to day information about Hex. Note down, evaluate day entry points of your liking. Remember the days Hex did I run up, or a special event that may of pumped or dumped Hex. With Hex Journal you have a personal digitized journal that belongs to you to keep track of your Hex t-shares and much more. Unravel the mechanical nature of how this programable product operates. By manually typing in the amount of t-shares you own the journal locks in the current data at time of entry and displays a past history of earlier entries. This layout gives the user a overview of the functionality of Hex. Your spread sheet reveals Hex in relationships to market pressure as well as showing its evolution in terms of adoption. The design of this data journal makes comparing past performance with present time performance for better future entry points, and aids the investor in understanding the complex nature of Hex. Analysis Hex in Uniswap version 2 and 3 brought and sold by long term and short term investors. Watch the movements of decentralized markets where liquidity volume and price fluctuates in a digital age of mad gains.

Description

1

Google sheet has three parts "About, Hex Journal and Charts". The "about page" explains terms, formulas and how to use notes. The "About" page shows a detail account on how to use the sheet. Sections with the color table in "note" makes it simple to organize the data. There is a explanation on how to use the columns and rows in a number format.

2

The sheet can expand and contract vertically and horizontally .There are 55 columns with 15 data entries that can be filled for optimizations. The table is divided into 3 sections. The first part extents from 1 to 19 with two expansions which deal which the Hex product. The first sections displays t-shares Hex operations and Hex price. Your t-shares is like a factory thats keeps producing more of itself. The more t-shares you own the more Hex interest you gain. The only input needed to tract your t-shares is the number the user writes in column 1,8. This number method avoids the exposure of your contract address by converting it into a number formate. The first expansion shows your personal t-shares performance (1-8).

3

The second expansion shows the collective over all performance of t-shares in pool. In this section the share rate shows that HEX share rate increases over time making it impossible to outperform long-term staking with a strategy of frequent short-time staking. Column 14 is total staking in pool with column 15 as allocated supply and the rest follows as the collective percentage in staking pool. The last columns 45-55 is a simulation of hypotheticals with 4 entry points. In column "AU18" you can experiment with APY which collects data from "AU5".Put number of t-shares you desire to speculate writing number of days in column"AU18". There are terms and definitions with a overview of charts far right.

4

Column "A" is your personal note section where user can interact with Hex Journal evaluation. Write down special events concerning that day, or note down your fundamental and technical analysis.

5

Row "2" in the About section are google notes that explains the headers. Google notes are discrete in size and are hard to see, you can find them on all cells in row "2" on top of right corner.

6

Row "3" is where links are found created by the Hex and Uniswap community. With just one click away the user can link directly without doing unnecessary time consuming work. The Uniswap links retrieve data source form uniswap.

Column "27" to "44" is uniswap data, this section must be filled manually. This section is optional.

Columns 45-55 display the definitions with number count. The number count looks at the chart overview found in AV10-52. The number count method prevent user from scrolling 45 columns horizontally, which is time consuming. Column AU18 is where the days go for estimated t-shares earning per share which is written on column AU5 in the simulation. There are 4 simulation with 4 imput data points to experiment with. The pink headers is where your input goes which manually has to be done since the price input can be of your choosing. There are 8 overview charts the first 3 charts concern are t-shares and price. The remaining 5 charts is Hex is in relation to Uniswap.
