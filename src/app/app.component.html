Introduction

Hex Journal is a google sheet data base interactive digital journal where you the user collect in a spreadsheet format day to day information about Hex. Note down, evaluate day entry points of your liking. Track Hex price, T-Shares, allocated supply, percentage staked, Hex/eth and much more. Unravel the mechanical nature of how this programable product operates by manually typing in the amount of T-Shares you own, the API does the rest (uniswap API not applicable in Hex Journal). Hex Journal locks in the current data at time of entry, and displays a past history of earlier entries, this layout gives the user a overview of the functionality of Hex. Your spreadsheet reveals Hex in relationship to market pressure, adoption etc. Connecting the API (Application Programming Interface) makes comparing past performance with today performance easy and functional. Lets begin to build Hex community together in this new age of digital mad gains.

Description

1

The google sheet has "3" pages (Journal, Charts and About). The Journal is your personal investment google sheet for the Hex. Charts is a fun way to see Hex in action with market pressure. The "about page" explains terms, definitions, formulas and notes. Explanations on how to use the columns and rows are in a number format.

2

The "Journal page" can expand and contract vertically and horizontally. There are 55 columns with 16 data entries. The table is divided into 3 sections. Part "1" extents from 1 to 19 with two expansions which deal which the Hex product. Sections "1" displays T-shares, Hex operations and Hex price. The only input needed to tract your T-shares is the number the user fills in column "1" and "8". This "number method avoids the exposure of your contract address by converting it into a number format. The first expansion shows your personal T-shares performance (1-8).

3

The second expansion shows the collective over all performance of T-shares in collective pool. In this section the share rate shows that Hex share rate increases over time making it impossible to outperform long-term staking with a strategy of frequent short-time staking. Column 14 is total staking in pool with column 15 as allocated supply and the rest follows as the collective percentage in staking pool. The last columns 45-55 is a simulation of hypotheticals with 4 entry points. In column "AU18" you can experiment with APY that collects data from "AU5. Put number of T-shares you desire to speculate "number of days in column AU18". In overview terms, definitions and charts are located far right.

4

Column "A" is your personal note section. Write down special events concerning that day. Notes is for fundamental and technical analysis.

5

Headers with notes are found in row "2". Google notes are discrete in size and are hard to see, you can find them on all cells in row "2" on top of right corner.

6

Row "3" is where links are found created by the Hex and the uniswap community. With just one click the user can link directly without doing unnecessary time consuming work. Uniswap links retrieve data source form uniswap version 2-3. Entry points must be manually executed.

7

Hex Journal "About sheet" underlines basic layout and functionality of how to access the Journal.

8

Column "43" is Hex ratio between liquidity and volume trading pairs. The lower the number the higher the volume. The higher the number the lower the volume.

9

Column "43" is the ratio between Uniswap 1-2-3 Total liquidity and volume with all erc-20 trading pairs. The lower the number the higher the volume. The higher the number the lower the volume.

Column "1" to "18" is the inner workings of Hex. Column "1" is amount of your T-shares (yours). The dark columns is for input use that is done manually, and where totals are shown. Pink headers is for input.

Column "27" to "44" is uniswap data, this section must be filled manually. This section is optional.

Columns 45-55 displays definitions in number count. The number count looks at the chart overview found in AV10-52. The number count method prevent user from scrolling 45 columns horizontally, which is time consuming. In simulation Column AU18 is where the days go for estimated T-shares earning per T-shares written on column AU5. . There are 4 simulations with 4 rows of input data points to experiment with. Pink headers is for input use. There are 8 overview charts the first 3 charts, T-shares and price. The remaining 5 charts is Hex in relation to uniswap.
