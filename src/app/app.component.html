      Description Hex  Journal

Google sheet has three parts "About, Hex Journal and Charts". The "about page" explains terms, formulas and notes on how to use Hex Journal. The "About" page shows a detail account on how to use this sheet. Sections with the color table in "note" makes it simple to organize the data. There is a explanation on how to use the columns and rows in a number format.

The sheet can expand and contract vertically and horizontally . There are 55 columns with 15 data entries that can be filled for optimizations. The table is divided into 3 sections. the first part extents from 1 to 19 with two expansions which deal which the Hex product. The first sections displays t-shares Hex operations and Hex price. Your t-shares is like a factory thats keeps producing more of itself, the more t-shares you own the more Hex interest you gain. The only input needed to tract your t-shares is the number the user writes in column 1,8. This number method avoids the exposure of your contract address by converting it into a number formate. The first expansion shows your personal t-shares performance (1-8).

The second expansion shows the collective over all performance of t-shares in pool. In this section the share rate shows that HEX share rate increases over time making it impossible to outperform long-term staking with a strategy of frequent short-time staking. Column 14 is total staking in pool with column 15 as allocated supply and the rest follows as the collective percentage in the staking pool. The last columns 45-55 is a simulation of hypotheticals with 4 entry points. In column "AU18" you can experiment with APY which collects data from "AU5".Put number of t-shares you desire to speculate with and write number of days in column"AU18". There are terms and definitions with a overview of charts far right.

Hex Journal is a composition of 3 parts, the head,body and legs. The head is part "1" (1-26). Part "1" is the inner workings of Hex,t-shares, share rate etc. The head is the thinking part of the Hex contract. The only input needed for the head section is column"1,8" (Total T-Shares Yours *). For basic use only the head is needed. The body is the Uniswap section which starts from column 27-44. This section shows in data format the exchange between sellers and buyers in Uniswap 2-3. The body is the liquidity and volume of Hex in Uniswap. The third part is the legs which is describes how the Hex Journal runs, its has a simulation with 4 hypotheticals,definition and terms and a overview of charts. ption with terms

Columns 45-55 display the definitions with number count. The number count looks at the chart overview found in AV10-52. The number count method prevent user from scrolling 45 columns horizontally, which is time consuming. Column AU18 is where the days go for estimated t-shares earning per share which is written on column AU5 in the simulation. There are 4 simulation with 4 imput data points to experiment with. The pink headers is where your input goes which manually has to be done since the price input can be of your choosing. There are 8 overview charts the first 3 charts concern are t-shares and price. The remaining 5 charts is Hex is in relation to Uniswap.

Column "A" is your personal note section where user can interact with Hex Journal for crypto evaluations. Write down special events concerning that day, or note down your fundamental or technical analysis.

Row "2" in the About section are google notes that explains the headers. Google notes are discrete in size and are hard to see, you can find them on all cells in row "2" on top of right corner.

Row "3" is where links are found created by the Hex and Uniswap community. With just one click away the user can link directly without doing unnecessary time consuming work. The Uniswap links show were information is retrieved if need be.

This is google sheet Hex Journal "About sheet" which underlines basic layout and functionality of how the Journal works.
